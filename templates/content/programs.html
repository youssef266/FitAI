Day 1: Chest and Triceps

1. Barbell Bench Press (4 sets of 8-12 reps)
2. Incline Dumbbell Press (3 sets of 10-15 reps)
3. Cable Fly (3 sets of 12-15 reps)
4. Tricep Pushdown (3 sets of 10-12 reps)
5. Overhead Dumbbell Extension (3 sets of 12-15 reps)
6. Tricep Dip (3 sets of 10-12 reps)

Day 2: Back and Biceps

1. Deadlift (4 sets of 8-12 reps)
2. Bent-Over Barbell Row (3 sets of 8-12 reps)
3. Pull-Up (3 sets of as many reps as possible)
4. Dumbbell Bicep Curl (3 sets of 10-12 reps)
5. Hammer Curl (3 sets of 10-12 reps)
6. Preacher Curl (3 sets of 10-12 reps)

Day 3: Legs

1. Squat (4 sets of 8-12 reps)
2. Leg Press (3 sets of 10-12 reps)
3. Lunges (3 sets of 10-12 reps per leg)
4. Leg Extension (3 sets of 12-15 reps)
5. Leg Curl (3 sets of 10-12 reps)
6. Calf Raise (3 sets of 12-15 reps)

Day 4: Shoulders and Abs

1. Standing Military Press (4 sets of 8-12 reps)
2. Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press (3 sets of 10-12 reps)
3. Lateral Raise (3 sets of 12-15 reps)
4. Reverse Fly (3 sets of 12-15 reps)
5. Hanging Leg Raise (3 sets of 10-12 reps)
6. Plank (3 sets of 30-60 seconds)

Day 5: Arms

1. Bench Dip (3 sets of 10-12 reps)
2. Barbell Curl (3 sets of 10-12 reps)
3. Hammer Curl (3 sets of 10-12 reps)
4. Tricep Extension (3 sets of 12-15 reps)
5. Cable Curl (3 sets of 10-12 reps)
6. Overhead Dumbbell Extension (3 sets of 12-15 reps)

Additional Tips

* Warm up with 5-10 minutes of cardio and dynamic stretching before each workout.
* Rest for 60-90 seconds between sets, and 120-180 seconds between exercises.
* Adjust the weight and reps based on your fitness level and goals.
* Increase the weight or reps every two weeks to continue progressive overload.
* Eat a caloric surplus of 250-500 calories above your maintenance level to support muscle growth.
* Get at least 7-8 hours of sleep per night to aid in recovery.
